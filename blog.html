Braiding Style Guide

Everything you need to know about our braiding styles — what they look like, how long they last, and how to care for them.

Knotless Braids

⏱ Install: 4 – 8 hours 💰 Starting at $200+ 📅 Lasts: 6 – 8 weeks

Knotless braids are the most popular braiding style right now — and for good reason. Unlike traditional box braids, knotless braids start with your natural hair and gradually add extensions using a feed-in technique. This means no bulky knot at the root, less tension on your scalp, and a flatter, more natural look.

They come in every size from jumbo to micro, and can be styled in any length from a trendy bob to waist-length. Knotless braids are lightweight, versatile, and perfect for anyone who wants a protective style that looks effortless.

Care Tips

  • Wrap your braids with a satin bonnet or scarf at night
  • Moisturize your scalp with a lightweight oil every 2–3 days
  • Avoid heavy products that cause buildup at the roots
  • Don't keep them in longer than 8 weeks to avoid matting

Best For

Everyone! Knotless braids work for all hair types and ages. They're especially great for first-timers or anyone with a sensitive scalp.

Boho Braids

⏱ Install: 5 – 9 hours 💰 Starting at $250+ 📅 Lasts: 6 – 8 weeks

Boho braids (also called bohemian braids or goddess braids) combine the neatness of knotless or box braids with loose, curly hair left out at the ends or woven throughout. The result is a soft, romantic, textured look that's perfect for any occasion.

You can get boho knotless, boho box braids, or boho locs — each variation offers a different vibe. The curly hair adds volume and movement that gives the braids a more relaxed, effortless feel compared to traditional braids.

Care Tips

  • Use a curl-refreshing spray to keep the loose curls defined
  • Gently detangle the curly sections with your fingers — never brush them
  • Sleep with braids in a loose pineapple or satin bonnet
  • Avoid getting the curly parts excessively wet

Best For

Anyone who wants a soft, feminine look. Boho braids are popular for weddings, vacations, and everyday glam. Great for all face shapes.

Box Braids

⏱ Install: 4 – 10 hours 💰 Starting at $200+ 📅 Lasts: 6 – 8 weeks

Box braids are a timeless, classic protective style. Each braid is sectioned into small square (box-shaped) parts and braided from root to tip with extensions. They've been a staple in Black hair culture for decades and remain one of the most requested styles.

Box braids can be done in any size — from chunky jumbo braids to ultra-fine micro braids. They can be worn long, short (bob), or anywhere in between. You can also accessorize them with beads, cuffs, or wraps.

Care Tips

  • Keep your scalp moisturized with a light oil or braid spray
  • Wash your braids every 2 weeks with diluted shampoo
  • Protect at night with a satin bonnet
  • Don't keep them in longer than 8 weeks

Best For

Everyone looking for a classic, low-maintenance protective style. Box braids are versatile — you can style them up, down, in a bun, or ponytail.

Cornrows

⏱ Install: 1 – 4 hours 💰 Starting at $200+ 📅 Lasts: 2 – 4 weeks

Cornrows are braids that lie flat against the scalp in straight rows, curves, or intricate designs. They're created using an underhand braiding technique and can be done with or without extensions. Cornrows have deep African roots and are one of the oldest braiding styles in history.

From simple straight-back cornrows to elaborate geometric patterns, stitch cornrows, and feed-in styles — the possibilities are endless. Cornrows are also the foundation for many other styles and can be dressed up or down.

Care Tips

  • Apply a light oil along the braids to prevent dryness
  • Wear a durag or satin scarf at night to keep them neat
  • Don't leave cornrows in too long — 2 to 4 weeks is ideal
  • Moisturize edges daily to prevent breakage

Best For

Men, women, and kids. Cornrows are perfect as a standalone style or as a base for wigs and weaves. Great for athletes and active lifestyles.

Twists

⏱ Install: 3 – 7 hours 💰 Starting at $200+ 📅 Lasts: 4 – 8 weeks

Twists are a beautiful, low-tension protective style created by twisting two or three strands of hair together. Popular variations include passion twists (which have a crimped, textured look) and Senegalese twists (which are sleek and rope-like).

Twists are generally faster to install than braids and put less stress on the hair. They come in all sizes and lengths, and the texture adds natural-looking volume and movement.

Care Tips

  • Moisturize with a light leave-in conditioner or oil
  • Sleep in a satin bonnet to prevent frizz
  • Avoid heavy manipulation — let them hang naturally
  • Refresh with a twist mousse if they start to unravel

Best For

Anyone wanting a softer, textured look. Twists are great for natural hair and work well for both casual and formal settings.

Lemonade Braids

⏱ Install: 2 – 5 hours 💰 Starting at $200+ 📅 Lasts: 2 – 4 weeks

Lemonade braids are side-swept cornrows made famous by Beyoncé in her "Lemonade" visual album. They're feed-in cornrows that go from one side of the head to the other, creating a sleek, angled look that's both glamorous and edgy.

These braids can be done in various sizes — from thin, intricate rows to thick, bold statements. They're often finished with long braided ends and can include designs or patterns near the hairline. Lemonade braids are a red-carpet favorite.

Care Tips

  • Keep edges moisturized with edge control and oil
  • Wrap with a silk scarf at night to preserve the style
  • Avoid heavy pulling or tight ponytails on the braided ends
  • Touch up edges if needed after 2 weeks

Best For

Anyone looking for a bold, statement style. Lemonade braids are perfect for events, photoshoots, and everyday glamour.

Faux Locs

⏱ Install: 5 – 10 hours 💰 Starting at $200+ 📅 Lasts: 6 – 8 weeks

Faux locs give you the look of real dreadlocks without the long-term commitment. They're created by wrapping hair extensions around individual braids to mimic the appearance and texture of mature locs. They come in a variety of textures — smooth, distressed, or boho.

Faux locs are a stunning protective style that lets you try the loc look temporarily. They can be done in any length and size, and styled in updos, buns, or worn freely. They're heavier than regular braids, so proper installation is important.

Care Tips

  • Keep your scalp clean with a diluted apple cider vinegar rinse
  • Moisturize your scalp regularly to prevent dryness
  • Sleep with a satin bonnet to reduce friction
  • Avoid excessive heat styling on the locs

Best For

Anyone who wants the look of locs without the commitment. Great for festivals, vacations, and making a bold style statement.

Butterfly Locs

⏱ Install: 5 – 9 hours 💰 Starting at $200+ 📅 Lasts: 6 – 8 weeks

Butterfly locs are a variation of faux locs with a more distressed, bohemian texture. They're created using the crochet method, wrapping loose, pre-looped hair around individual braids to create a messy, lived-in loc look with loops and curls peeking out.

The "butterfly" name comes from the loopy, airy texture that resembles butterfly wings. They're lighter than traditional faux locs, easier to install, and have a gorgeous, effortlessly cool appearance.

Care Tips

  • Keep your scalp moisturized with a light oil spray
  • Don't over-manipulate — let the messy texture do its thing
  • Protect at night with a satin bonnet or pillowcase
  • Refresh with a light holding spray if loops flatten

Best For

Anyone wanting a trendy, effortless look. Butterfly locs are perfect for those who love the boho aesthetic with a loc twist.

Jumbo Braids

⏱ Install: 2 – 4 hours 💰 Starting at $200+ 📅 Lasts: 4 – 6 weeks

Jumbo braids are thick, chunky braids that make a bold fashion statement. They require fewer braids overall (usually 10–20), which means a much faster install time compared to smaller sizes. They can be done as jumbo box braids, jumbo knotless, or jumbo twists.

Jumbo braids are lightweight, trendy, and perfect for anyone who wants a quick protective style. They're also a great option for beginners or anyone who doesn't want to sit for hours. The large size makes them easy to style in buns, ponytails, or half-up looks.

Care Tips

  • Be gentle — jumbo braids can unravel faster due to their size
  • Moisturize your scalp every few days
  • Sleep with a satin scarf to prevent frizz
  • Keep install time to 4–6 weeks max

Best For

Anyone wanting a quick, trendy protective style. Great for busy schedules, vacation prep, or a bold new look without a long appointment.

Microlocs

⏱ Install: 8 – 15 hours 💰 Starting at $400+ 📅 Lasts: 8+ weeks

Microlocs (also called sisterlocks or micro locs) are very small, thin locs that give a detailed, fine-textured look. They offer more versatility than traditional locs because the small size allows you to style them like loose hair — in ponytails, buns, updos, or worn freely.

Installing microlocs is a long process due to the tiny sections and precision required. However, the result is a stunning, long-lasting style that grows with your natural hair if maintained properly.

Care Tips

  • Keep scalp clean and moisturized with a light spray
  • Re-twist or maintain every 4–6 weeks
  • Avoid heavy products that cause buildup
  • Be patient — microlocs take time to mature and settle

Best For

Anyone committed to a long-term loc journey with a refined, polished look. Microlocs are versatile and work for both professional and casual settings.

Natural Cornrows Hair

⏱ Install: 30 min – 2 hours 💰 Starting at $50+ 📅 Lasts: 1 – 3 weeks

Natural cornrows are simple braiding designs done using only your natural hair — no extensions added. They're perfect for everyday wear, as a base style under wigs, or for kids who need a quick, neat, and protective hairstyle.

These include basic straight-back cornrows, two-strand flat braids, and simple patterns. Because no extensions are used, the installation is faster and gentler on the hair and scalp.

Care Tips

  • Moisturize your scalp and braids daily with a light oil
  • Cover with a satin scarf or bonnet at night
  • Don't leave in too long — re-do every 1–3 weeks
  • Keep edges protected with edge control

Best For

Kids, athletes, busy professionals, and anyone wanting a quick, clean, natural look. Also great as a protective base under wigs and weaves.

Wig Cornrows (Braiding for Wigs)

⏱ Install: 1 – 3 hours 💰 Starting at $180+ 📅 Lasts: 2 – 4 weeks

Wig cornrows are flat braiding patterns done on your natural hair to create a smooth, secure base for wig installation. The goal is to braid the hair as flat as possible so your wig sits naturally and comfortably on top.

Common patterns include straight-back cornrows, circle braids, or crisscross patterns depending on your wig type (lace front, full wig, etc.). A good braid-down is essential for a natural-looking wig install and protects your hair underneath.

Care Tips

  • Keep your braids and scalp clean under the wig
  • Moisturize your natural hair before braiding
  • Don't wear the same wig braid pattern for more than 4 weeks
  • Give your hair a break between wig installs

Best For

Wig wearers who need a flat, secure base. Essential for lace fronts, U-part wigs, and full wigs. Protects your natural hair while wearing wigs.

Men's Braids

⏱ Install: 1 – 3 hours 💰 Starting at $80+ 📅 Lasts: 2 – 4 weeks

Men's braiding styles include straight-back cornrows, stitch braids, feed-in braids, two-strand twists, and braided designs with fades. Men's braids are typically cornrow-based and lie flat against the scalp for a clean, sharp look.

Popular options include straight-backs for a classic look, stitch cornrows for a modern twist, and intricate patterns or designs for those wanting something unique. Men's braids are a great way to keep hair neat and styled.

Care Tips

  • Wear a durag or wave cap at night to preserve the style
  • Moisturize your scalp daily with a light oil
  • Keep the style for 2–4 weeks maximum
  • Maintain clean edges with regular touch-ups

Best For

Men and boys of all ages. Whether you want a classic, clean look or an edgy design, men's braids are versatile and easy to maintain.

Kid Braids

⏱ Install: 1 – 4 hours 💰 Starting at $200+ 📅 Lasts: 2 – 4 weeks

Kid braids are gentle, age-appropriate braiding styles designed for children. We offer kids' cornrows, kids' box braids, and kids' twists. Every style is done with care to avoid excessive tension on young, delicate hair and scalps.

Kids' braids can include fun elements like beads, colorful accessories, and creative patterns. They're a great way to keep children's hair protected, neat, and stylish for school, activities, and special occasions.

Care Tips

  • Keep the braids loose — never too tight for children
  • Moisturize the scalp with kid-friendly oils
  • Use a satin pillowcase or bonnet at night
  • Don't keep styles in longer than 2–4 weeks for kids

Best For

Children of all ages who need a neat, protective style. Perfect for school, summer, vacations, and keeping hair manageable.

Feed-in Braids

⏱ Install: 2 – 5 hours 💰 Starting at $200+ 📅 Lasts: 2 – 4 weeks

Feed-in braids are a technique where braiding hair is gradually added to create a seamless, natural-looking braid that starts thin at the hairline and gets thicker as it goes. This reduces tension at the roots and gives a cleaner, flatter finish compared to traditional braids.

Feed-in braids are commonly used for cornrow styles, ponytails, and lemonade braids. The gradual feed-in technique makes the braids look like they're growing directly from the scalp, giving a polished, professional appearance.

Care Tips

  • Keep edges moisturized with oil or edge control
  • Wrap with a silk or satin scarf at night
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on the braids
  • Touch up edges after 2 weeks if needed

Best For

Anyone wanting a sleek, polished cornrow style. Feed-in braids are perfect for special events, everyday wear, and anyone who prefers less tension on the hairline.

Ponytails

⏱ Install: 2 – 4 hours 💰 Starting at $200+ 📅 Lasts: 2 – 3 weeks

Braided ponytails combine the beauty of cornrows or feed-in braids with a sleek ponytail finish. The front section is braided flat against the scalp, then gathered into a high, mid, or low ponytail. Extensions can be added for length and fullness.

Ponytail styles range from simple straight-back cornrows into a sleek pony to elaborate feed-in designs that flow into a dramatic braided or curly ponytail. They're elegant, versatile, and perfect for any occasion.

Care Tips

  • Don't tie the ponytail too tight to avoid tension
  • Moisturize the braided sections and edges daily
  • Wrap with a silk scarf at night
  • Refresh the ponytail with mousse or gel as needed

Best For

Anyone wanting an elegant, pulled-together look. Ponytails are great for events, work, workouts, and everyday style.
